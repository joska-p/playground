## The Emergence of Complexity

This is an invitation to a walk. At every crossroads we will ask the same question: **what if we reversed this?** It is this question, asked again and again, that builds the whole architecture of numbers from the ground up.

## Step 01 — ℕ

## The Natural Numbers

**The Question**

- What if something existed?

We must begin somewhere. Mathematics starts from an idea of disarming simplicity: that _something exists_. That something is $1$.

We add a rule of motion:
- **If a number exists, its successor — that number plus one — also exists.**

```latex
Apply it once     1 + 1 = 2
Again             2 + 1 = 3
Then              4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, toward infinity
```

- The mechanism is as simple as it is tireless.

**The Consequence**

- From one brick and one generative rule, an infinite set emerges.
- **Multiplication** was never a new rule.
- It is a compact notation for repeated addition, already contained in the successor from the start.

## Step 02 — ℤ

## Zero and Negative Numbers

**The Question**

- Can we undo an addition?

If we can add, we ask: _can we invert the operation?_

Walk forward 3 steps and return to the starting point

- What operation does that describe?

We invent subtraction.

```latex
For a while, everything is fine: 5 - 2 = 3, 7 - 4 = 3
```

All results land in $\mathbb{N}$.

```latex
Then someone dares to write 3 - 3. Silence.
```

The result _should_ exist.<br />
We move forward 3, we move back 3, we return to the origin.<br />
But $\mathbb{N}$ has **no symbol for nothing**.

```latex
And 3 - 5 is worse: the answer lives nowhere in our universe.
```

The universe is not **closed** under subtraction.

>We have only one option: enlarge it. Not by choice — by logical necessity. We invent:

1. **Creation of zero**, 0

```latex
the solution of n - n = 0. The neutral element, the return to nothing.
```

2. **Appearance of negative numbers**

```latex
so that 3 - 5 = -2 has somewhere to live.
```

**The Consequence**

- The number line undergoes a foundational symmetry break. It has no beginning anymore. It extends infinitely in both directions, with zero as the center around which every positive has its perfect negative mirror. The integers $\mathbb{Z}$ are born — finally closed under subtraction.

## Step 03 — ℚ

## The Rational Numbers

**The Question**

- Can we undo a multiplication?

The same reasoning repeats.

- If we can multiply, can we _undo_ a multiplication?

We invent division.

```latex
Simple examples: 6 ÷ 2 = 3, 10 ÷ 5 = 2
```

Everything is fine as long as the result remains integer.

```latex
But 1 ÷ 2 has no integer answer.
```

No integer multiplied by 2 gives 1.

The universe is not closed, once again.

We must extend the universe.

```latex
Appearance of rationals: a/b
```

**The Consequence**

- The number line becomes dense.
- Between two integers, an infinity of rationals.
- ℚ is born — closed under division.

> To feel it:
> between 1/2 and 1/3, take the average 5/12.
> Between 1/2 and 5/12, take 11/24.
> The game continues.

## Step 04 — log

## Taming the Explosion — Logarithms

**The Question**

- Given the result, what exponent produced it?

Repeated multiplication becomes exponentiation.

```latex
Definition: a^n = a × a × ... × a  (n times)
```

Growth can become staggering.

```latex
Illustrative table:
n    10^n
1    10
3    1,000
6    1,000,000
9    1,000,000,000
12   1,000,000,000,000
```

We ask for the inverse.

```latex
Solve: find x such that 10^x = 1000
```

**The Consequence**

- Birth of the logarithm.
- Multiplication becomes addition via the log.

```latex
Key formula: log(A × B) = log(A) + log(B)
```

Logarithmic scales make the unmeasurable readable.

```latex
Example: log_{10}(1000) = 3
```

## Step 05 — i

## The Wall of the Impossible

**The Question**

- What number, squared, gives -1?

The reals ℝ fill the line.

```latex
Famous constants: √2, π, e
```

But the line enforces a law: every real square ≥ 0.

```latex
Impossible equation: x^2 + 1 = 0
```

No real solution.

Mathematicians chose to act.

```latex
Postulate: existence of a number i such that i^2 = -1
```

**The Consequence**

- Introduction of the imaginary.
- A new alphabet to solve the unsolvable.

## The Complex Plane — A Two-Dimensional Revolution

**The Question**

- Where does i go?

i does not sit on the line.

We build a second axis.

- Multiplication reads as: rotation + scaling.
- Multiplying by i = rotation by 90°.

## Epilogue — e^"{"iπ"}" + 1 = 0

## Euler's Formula — The Unlikely Convergence

Five symbols meet.

```latex
0, 1, π, e, i
```

They come from different horizons and converge.

```latex
Formula: e^{iπ} + 1 = 0
```

**The Consequence**

- An elegant synthesis of algebra, geometry, and analysis.
- An invitation to continue the walk.

>Each step shows an internal logic: pose an operation, notice a missing closure, extend the universe by postulate or construction, then understand the new algebraic geometry that follows.
